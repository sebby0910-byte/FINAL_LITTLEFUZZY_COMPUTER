CHAPTER 10 — RESOLUTION — FINAL RECORD
[ CLOSE FILE ]
Year 2230. Thirteen years after first contact. The Hesperus Archive was sealed. No further transmissions authorized. No further observation permitted. The Fuzzies had rebuilt. Not as they were. As they chose to be. Their societies were small. Decentralized. Resilient. No universal doctrine. No absolute authority. Cultural diversity preserved. Memory maintained through story. Knowledge transmitted through practice. Mistakes remembered. Not erased. Human records documented every intervention. Every error. Every assumption. Every failure. The Contact Initiative was formally dissolved. New guidelines enacted. Non-interference classified as primary doctrine. Future encounters would proceed with restraint. With patience. With humility. Hesperus-4 was designated a protected system. Permanent exclusion zone. No commercial access. No military presence. No experimental deployments. Only passive astronomical monitoring. In private correspondence, lead researchers expressed regret. Not for discovery. For intrusion. For haste. For arrogance. Humanity had learned. At a cost. The Fuzzies would never know how close they came to extinction. And humanity would never forget how close it came to becoming the thing it feared. Contact was not conquest. Knowledge was not ownership. Observation was not innocence. The archive closed with a single directive: Remember.
